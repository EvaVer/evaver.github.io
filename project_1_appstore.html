Google PlayStore Advice

A python project giving advice to a young start-up wanting to create a new app

This project is still under development!

In a notebook, two datasets containing data from the Google Play Store were inspected, cleaned, and analysed. Data analysis was performed on the most relevant data in order to give a substantiated advice regarding the creation and launching of one or more new apps on the Google Play Store.

Description of the datasets.

The first dataset is 1,4 MB large, and contains data on all apps on the Google AppStore by [date]. It holds data on the app size, the number of downloads, app price, app category, and more.

The second dataset was less important. This dataset was 7,7 MB large, and contains data on the reviews of the apps in the google AppStore. Every line is a seperate review, containing a rating, the translated review, and sentiment polarity (whether the review used positive or negative words)and sentiment subjectivity (strength of the review).

Column 'Size' to integer datatype

The column size contains both numeric and letter values. An example of a value is '19M'. In order to change this data type to an integer, the M and K's need to be removed from the string, and the numeric values multiplied by either 1000 (K) or 1,000,000 (M).

# Remove all non-numeric values in each number

size = app_data["Size"]
new_size_list = []

# Loop over every value in the series.
# Extract the numeric values from the series and change M's into 1000000's and K's into 1000's.
for value in size:
    new_object = 0
    
    if value == 'Varies with device':
        new_object = float('NaN')
    elif value == '':
        new_object = float('NaN')
    else:
        size_letter = ''
        numeric_value = 0
    
        # get size (letter, is 'Kb' or 'Mb')
        size_letter = value[-1].lower()
        
        # get the numeric value
        numeric_values = float(value[:-1])
        
        # multiply numeric value with size (letter)
        if size_letter == 'k':
            numeric_values = numeric_values * 1000     #K is 1000
        elif size_letter == 'm':
            numeric_values = numeric_values * 1000000  #M is 1.000.000
            
        new_object = int(numeric_values)
        
	# add to the list
    new_size_list.append(new_object)

# Overwrite the existing column with the new list.
app_data["Size"] = new_size_list

# Cast the column values as floats
app_data["Size"] = app_data["Size"].astype(float)

Duplicate values

It is important to check that all app names are unique, otherwise you run the risk of analysing the same app multiple times, which will introduce bias.

Analysis showed a significant number of duplicate rows with the same app name. Rows that were completely identical were removed from the dataset. Some rows had the same app name but had slight differences in app size, rating, and number of downloads. It was decided to keep one row per app and app category, since app category is one of the main elements of the later data analysis

Final Advice

The current Google Play Store app market is filled with a wide choice variety and almost 10.000 apps. The app store data of previous years was analysed with a focus on the most succesful app categories and target audience, and paid and free apps were compared. This data is most valuable to create a business plan for a new app startup company

Apps from the app categories communication, video players, social, entertainment, and photography are downloaded most often (figure 1). This implies that these categories are popular among smartphone users. Other popular categories include productivity, game, travel and local, tools, and news and magazines. The app categories family, game, and tools hold significantly more apps than other categories. Similarly, while the app categories communication and social are large, most of these apps connect to large established platforms such as Whatsapp, Instagram, Google Hangouts, and more. The market for the app categories family, games, tools, communication and social may be saturated already, and therefore my recommendation is to focus on another popular categories such as entertainment, photography, or productivity.

Analysis of the content rating showed that the most app installs were in the categories everyone, everyone 10+ and the category teen (figure 5). The highest number of installs were in the category teen, and therefore it is recommended to focus on this target audience. From the 10 categories with most installs, teens installed most apps from the categories video players, socials, news and magazines, and entertainment.

Lastly, as seen in figure 7, the total number of installs is substantially higher for free apps. Consumers might be more willing to try a new free app on the Play Store. Therefore, it is recommended to create a free app with possible ads or in-app purchases, in order to make the product profitable.

In conclusion, the Google Play Store is filled with many apps, but there is still space for new apps with new functionality. My recommendation is to create a free app targeted at teens in one of the popular app categories such as entertainment, photography, productivity, tools, or game.
